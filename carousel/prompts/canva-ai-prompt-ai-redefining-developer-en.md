## How to use this prompt

1. Go to canva.com and create a new design.
2. Select "Instagram Post (Square)" — 1080x1080px.
3. Click the "Magic Design" or "Magic Write" button, or
   open the AI assistant panel.
4. Paste each slide prompt one at a time. After Canva
   generates a slide, duplicate the page and paste the
   next slide prompt.
5. Alternatively, paste the full prompt below into Canva's
   Bulk Create or Magic Design if you want all 8 slides
   generated at once.

---

## Full prompt (paste into Canva Magic Design or AI chat)

Create an 8-page Instagram carousel (1080x1080px per page)
for a technical content brand called t0k1dev. The design
should be typography-only — no illustrations, no stock
photos, no icons. Dark and minimal aesthetic.

**Brand style**
- Background: very dark, near-black on all pages
- Accent color: violet/purple
- Text: white or off-white for main copy, gray for secondary
  text and source citations
- Font: modern geometric sans-serif (Inter, Space Grotesk,
  or similar). Bold for headlines, regular for body.
- Generous spacing. Clean and minimal. Let the text breathe.
- Every page must show "@t0k1dev" small in the bottom-left
  corner in gray.

---

**Page 1 — Cover**
Dark background. Two lines of text, large and centered:
Line 1: "The job title is the same."
Line 2: "The job is not."
Make the second line bold and in purple/violet. It should
be the dominant visual on the page. Nothing else except
"@t0k1dev" small at the bottom-left.

---

**Page 2 — The pattern**
Dark background. Three lines of text, left-aligned,
stacked with good spacing:
"npm felt like cheating. Then everyone had it."
"Cloud felt like cheating. Then everyone had it."
"AI feels like cheating. You know where this goes."
Make the last line bold or in the accent color. All three
lines should be readable at a glance.

---

**Page 3 — The uncomfortable truth**
Dark background. Stat-forward layout:
- Very large "70%" in violet/purple at the top, dominant
- Below it in white: "of developer time goes to maintenance,
  debugging, and technical debt — not building new things."
- Below that in gray, smaller: "Most of the code we wrote
  was assembly of known parts. AI just does that faster now."
- Small gray text at the bottom: "Stripe, survey of 11,000+
  developers"

---

**Page 4 — The bottleneck moved**
Dark background. Layered text hierarchy:
- Top: "Copilot users finish tasks 55% faster." — bold,
  "55% faster" in violet
- Middle: "But writing code was already a minority of the
  workday." — regular weight, slightly smaller
- Bottom: two-line punchline in violet, bold:
  "AI didn't speed up the work.
  It showed which part was always the hard part."
- Small gray text: "GitHub Copilot study, 2022"

---

**Page 5 — What changed**
Dark background. Clean stacked list:
Four lines in white, regular weight, left-aligned:
"You write less code."
"You make more decisions."
"You review output you didn't write."
"You define constraints before anything exists."
A thin divider line below the list.
Below the divider, bold violet text, larger:
"The job didn't disappear. It moved upstream."

---

**Page 6 — One dev outships a team**
Dark background. Story-first layout:
- Body text in white: "A developer I know shipped a full
  SaaS in 6 weeks. Alone. Auth, billing, dashboard,
  background jobs, API."
- Second line: "Not a prodigy. Just full context and no
  alignment meetings."
- Bold closing line in violet: "46% of code in
  Copilot-enabled files is now AI-generated."
- Small gray text: "GitHub Octoverse, 2023"

---

**Page 7 — Skills**
Dark background split into two sections with a visible
divider or contrasting background tones.

Left or top section — slightly lighter dark background:
- Label in violet uppercase: "GAINING VALUE"
- Three items in white:
  "— System design"
  "— Critical code review"
  "— Writing precise problem descriptions"

Right or bottom section — default dark background:
- Label in gray uppercase: "LOSING VALUE"
- Three items in gray, slightly dimmer:
  "— Memorizing syntax"
  "— Writing boilerplate"
  "— Configuring standard tooling"

---

**Page 8 — CTA**
Dark background. Softer, closing tone:
- Body text in white, four lines:
  "Pick one real project.
  Use AI on it every day for a month.
  Not to generate everything —
  to learn when to trust it and when to throw it away."
- Bold violet line below, with spacing above it:
  "The shift already happened. Work with it deliberately."
- Footer in gray, small, at the bottom:
  "tokidev.com · @t0k1dev"
