## Figma Make Prompt

Paste the following prompt directly into Figma Make.

---

Create an 8-slide carousel for Instagram and LinkedIn. Each
slide is a 1080x1080px square frame. Name the frames Slide 1
through Slide 8.

**Brand identity**
- Name: t0k1dev
- Handle: @t0k1dev
- Website: tokidev.com
- Voice: direct, technical, no fluff

**Color palette**
- Background: #0D0D0D (near black)
- Primary accent: #7C3AED (violet/purple)
- Secondary accent: #A78BFA (light purple)
- Body text: #E5E5E5 (off-white)
- Muted text: #737373 (gray, for sources and secondary copy)
- Highlight block: #1A1A2E (dark navy, for contrast sections)

**Typography**
- Headlines: Inter Bold or Space Grotesk Bold, 48–72px
- Body: Inter Regular or Space Grotesk Regular, 24–32px
- Labels/sources: Inter Regular, 16–18px, muted text color
- All text left-aligned unless centered treatment is
  explicitly noted

**Layout rules**
- 80px padding on all sides of every slide
- Consistent placement of @t0k1dev handle bottom-left,
  16–18px, muted text color, on every slide
- Slide number top-right, small, muted text color
- No stock photos, no illustrations, no icons
- Typography-only design — the copy is the visual

---

### Slide 1 — Cover

**Headline (large, dominant, centered):**
The job title is the same.
The job is not.

**Treatment:** "The job is not." in primary accent color
(#7C3AED) or white with higher weight. Maximum font size.
Full bleed dark background. Nothing else on the slide
except the handle at the bottom.

---

### Slide 2 — The pattern

**Body (stacked, left-aligned, equal weight):**
npm felt like cheating. Then everyone had it.
Cloud felt like cheating. Then everyone had it.
AI feels like cheating. You know where this goes.

**Treatment:** Last line in accent color or bold. All three
lines same size (~40px). Generous line height.

---

### Slide 3 — The uncomfortable truth

**Stat (very large, dominant):**
70%

**Subhead:**
of developer time goes to maintenance, debugging, and
technical debt — not building new things.

**Body:**
Most of the code we wrote was assembly of known parts.
AI just does that faster now.

**Source (small, muted):**
Stripe, survey of 11,000+ developers

**Treatment:** "70%" in accent color, 120–144px. Subhead
directly below at 28px. Body copy below that at 24px,
slightly reduced opacity. Source at bottom, 16px, muted.

---

### Slide 4 — Bottleneck moved

**Stat (large):**
55% faster

**Subhead:**
Copilot users finish the same tasks in significantly
less time. (GitHub, 2022)

**Body:**
But writing code was already a minority of the workday.

**Punchline (bold, accent color):**
AI didn't speed up the work.
It showed which part was always the hard part.

**Treatment:** Stat large at top, subhead below at 24px
muted, body below at 28px, punchline at bottom in accent
color bold. Source inline with subhead.

---

### Slide 5 — What changed

**List (stacked, left-aligned, body weight):**
You write less code.
You make more decisions.
You review output you didn't write.
You define constraints before anything exists.

**Summary line (bold, accent color, larger):**
The job didn't disappear. It moved upstream.

**Treatment:** Four lines at 32px with generous spacing.
A horizontal rule or spacing gap between the list and the
summary line. Summary line at 36–40px bold in accent color.

---

### Slide 6 — Solo dev

**Body (left-aligned, story lead):**
A developer I know shipped a full SaaS in 6 weeks. Alone.
Auth, billing, dashboard, background jobs, API.

Not a prodigy. Just full context and no alignment meetings.

**Stat (bold, accent color):**
46% of code in Copilot-enabled files is now AI-generated.

**Source (small, muted):**
GitHub Octoverse, 2023

**Treatment:** Body text at 28px, normal weight. Stat at
36px bold in accent color. Source at 16px muted below stat.

---

### Slide 7 — Skills

**Left block or top block:**
GAINING VALUE  ← label, 18px, accent color, uppercase
— System design
— Critical code review
— Writing precise problem descriptions

**Right block or bottom block:**
LOSING VALUE  ← label, 18px, muted color, uppercase
— Memorizing syntax
— Writing boilerplate
— Configuring standard tooling

**Treatment:** Two visually distinct blocks. Gaining block
on a slightly lighter dark background (#1A1A2E). Losing
block on default background with reduced opacity text.
Dividing line or gap between them. List items at 26px,
no bullet characters — use em dash (—) as prefix.

---

### Slide 8 — CTA

**Body:**
Pick one real project.
Use AI on it every day for a month.
Not to generate everything —
to learn when to trust it and when to throw it away.

**Takeaway (bold, accent color):**
The shift already happened. Work with it deliberately.

**Footer:**
tokidev.com · @t0k1dev

**Treatment:** Body at 28px normal weight. Takeaway at
32px bold accent color with a top margin separating it
from the body. Footer at 18px muted at the bottom center
or bottom left.

---

## Notes for Figma Make

- Apply the same slide number (top-right, muted, 16px) and
  @t0k1dev handle (bottom-left, muted, 16px) to all 8
  frames as a consistent element.
- Keep at least 80px of breathing room between text blocks.
- Do not add decorative elements, gradients, or textures
  unless they directly support the typographic hierarchy.
- If Inter is not available, use Space Grotesk as the
  primary fallback. If neither is available, use any
  geometric sans-serif (Outfit, DM Sans, Poppins).
